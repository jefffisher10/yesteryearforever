LAYER 1

Surface

What I do in the visible world.

Clinical engineering. Electronics. Troubleshooting. Repair. Writing. Music. Systems. Documentation. Problem solving. Building things that work.

I grew up on a farm. I’ve worked in electronics engineering, startup operations, and medical equipment service. I’ve helped build businesses, repair machines, write procedures, design processes, and keep complicated things running.

I am at home with tools, wires, dirt, diagrams, checklists, signal paths, quiet rooms, and long projects.

LAYER 2

Systems

How I tend to move through problems.

Understand the system.
Find the fault line.
Repair what is broken.
Rebuild what can be improved.
Document the pattern.
Repeat.

I learn by making. By fixing. By tracing causes. By watching how parts interact. The fastest way for me to understand something has usually been to build it, test it, or take it apart.

This has shown up in electronics repair, self-hosted web infrastructure, robotics, weather instrumentation, observatory building, studio construction, scripts, archive systems, and countless smaller projects done purely out of curiosity.

LAYER 3

Pattern

How I think about identity and continuity.

I have long been fascinated by the idea that identity may be less about fixed substance and more about structure, continuity, and arrangement — patterns persisting through change.

Maybe that is one reason I am drawn to systems, archives, journals, diagrams, modular design, and old machines: they all preserve traces of pattern across time.

Lego as a child. Nature cross-sections on the wall. Handwritten journals for decades. Typewriters. Schematics. Maps. Procedures. Websites built to last.

LAYER 4

Reality

The deeper questions underneath the projects.

I am drawn to questions about what is actually there beneath appearance: perception versus reality, mechanism versus mystery, identity versus change, and how meaning survives inside a scientific picture of the world.

Science has never reduced wonder for me. It has sharpened it. The hidden structure is usually stranger, larger, and more beautiful than the surface impression.

I have always loved the ocean, astronomy, deep time, nature, machinery, and the feeling that reality is bigger than our first explanation of it.

LAYER 5

Experience

What keeps this from becoming only abstraction.

Rationality, humility, honesty, and truth matter deeply to me. But feelings matter too. Maybe more than we often admit.

People may forget the exact words. They often remember how they felt. That matters. It changes how I think about meaning, memory, religion, art, music, family, and everyday life.

Running has been one way I’ve lived this out: long, solitary miles for no audience, no race, no external reward — just because doing it feels true. Handwritten journals are another. So is music.

I am interested in systems, but I don’t want to lose the human inside them.
